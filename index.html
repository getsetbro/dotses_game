Dotses

Connect same-color dots to clear them from the board and score points.

How to Play

Connecting dots

Drag your finger across the board to draw a path between dots of the same color. Lift your finger to commit the move — all dots in your path are cleared from the board and you score one point per dot.

  • You must connect at least two dots.
  • Your path must stay on the same color.
  • You can only move to dots that are directly above, below, left, or right of your current dot (no diagonals).
  • You can backtrack by dragging back over your previous dot.

Closing a loop

If you drag your path back to any dot already in your path, you close a loop. When you lift your finger after closing a loop:

  • Every dot of that color on the entire board is cleared, not just the ones in your path.
  • You score one point per dot removed.

Closing a loop is the most powerful move in the game — aim for colors with many dots on the board.

Encircling dots

When you close a loop, any dots of other colors that are completely enclosed inside your loop are also cleared as a bonus. You score one point per encircled dot.

Draw your loop around a pocket of the board that contains dots you want to clear. The more dots you enclose, the higher your bonus.

Game Modes

Moves

You have 30 moves to score as many points as possible. Every time you lift your finger and commit a move, your move count goes down by one. The game ends when you reach zero.

Daily Challenge

A fixed puzzle — the same for everyone on the same day. Your score is submitted to the global leaderboard. You can only play the Daily Challenge once per day.

Endless (coming soon)

Play without a move limit.

Power-Ups

Power-UpHow to use
Clear a ColorTap the button, then tap any dot on the board. Every dot of that color is instantly removed. Does not cost a move.
Add 5 MovesAdds five moves to your remaining count. Can only be used once per game.
Remove Single DotDouble-tap any dot on the board to remove just that one dot.

Tips

  • Save the Clear a Color power-up for a color with many dots on the board.
  • Plan loops that encircle clusters of a different color for big bonus clears.
  • In Moves mode, longer paths score the same points per dot as short paths — but closing a loop can clear an entire color in one move, which is far more efficient.
  • After a loop clear, dots of that color are temporarily prevented from spawning in the refill, giving you more variety on the next turn.
